## Language

- **British English** spellings throughout (colour, organised, behaviour, analyse)
- **Active voice** preferred: "The planner generates a timeline" not "A timeline is generated by the planner"
- **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence has two commas and a semicolon, split it up.
- **Avoid jargon** unless the audience explicitly expects it. When in doubt, use the plain word.
- **Contractions are fine** in marketing copy and documentation (it's, you'll, we've). Avoid in legal or formal compliance text.

## Capitalisation

- **Page titles and H1s:** Title Case (e.g. "Campaign Launch Pipeline")
- **H2s and below:** Sentence case (e.g. "Audience segmentation settings")
- **UI labels and buttons:** Sentence case (e.g. "Create new campaign")
- **Product name:** Always "skrptiq" in lowercase, even at the start of a sentence. Never "Skrptiq" or "SKRPTIQ".
- **Feature names:** Lowercase unless they are a branded proper noun (e.g. "audience segmentation", not "Audience Segmentation")

## Punctuation

- **Oxford comma:** Always. "Strategy, creative, and media" not "Strategy, creative and media."
- **Em dashes:** Use sparingly for emphasis or asides — no spaces either side. Do not use double hyphens (--) as a substitute.
- **Ellipsis:** Three dots with no spaces before (e.g. "Loading..."). Never use to trail off vaguely in marketing copy.
- **Full stops in lists:** If list items are full sentences, end each with a full stop. If they are fragments, no full stop.
- **Exclamation marks:** Maximum one per page. Never more than one in a row. Never in headings.
- **Quotation marks:** Double quotes for direct speech and UI label references. Single quotes for terms used in a non-standard way.

## Formatting

- **Bullet lists:** For unordered items, related but not sequential. Start each item with a capital letter.
- **Numbered lists:** Only for sequential steps where order matters. Use "1. 2. 3." not "Step 1, Step 2."
- **Bold:** For key terms on first introduction, UI element names, and emphasis. Do not bold entire sentences.
- **Italic:** For publication titles, foreign words, and placeholder text. Not for emphasis (use bold instead).
- **CTA formatting:** Calls to action use sentence case and an action verb. "Start your campaign" not "START YOUR CAMPAIGN" or "Click Here To Begin."

## Links and References

- **Link text must be descriptive.** "Read the audience segmentation guide" not "Click here" or "Learn more."
- **Never use raw URLs** in body text. Always wrap in descriptive link text.
- **External links** open in a new tab. Internal links navigate in the same tab.
- **Anchor text length:** Aim for three to eight words. Avoid linking entire sentences.

## Numbers and Data

- **Spell out** one to nine. Use digits for 10 and above.
- **Percentages:** Use the % symbol with no space (e.g. 25%, not 25 % or twenty-five percent).
- **Currency:** Symbol before the number, no space (e.g. £500, $1,200). Use commas as thousands separators.
- **Dates:** Day Month Year with no ordinal suffix (e.g. 14 March 2025, not March 14th, 2025).
- **Times:** 24-hour format with a colon (e.g. 14:30, not 2:30 PM) unless writing for a US-primary audience.
- **Ranges:** Use an en dash with no spaces (e.g. 10--20, 2024--2025).

## Abbreviations and Acronyms

- **Spell out on first use**, then abbreviate in parentheses: "key performance indicator (KPI)."
- After first use, the abbreviation alone is fine for the rest of the document.
- **Common exceptions** that never need spelling out: UK, US, URL, API, PDF, CTA, SEO.
- **Do not create new abbreviations** unless they will be used at least three times in the same document.

## Images and Visual Content

- **Alt text is mandatory** for every image. Describe what the image shows, not what it is ("Bar chart comparing Q1 and Q2 conversion rates" not "Chart" or "Image").
- **Captions:** Sentence case, ending with a full stop. Keep to one sentence. Format: descriptive text, not a repeat of the heading.
- **File naming:** Lowercase, hyphens as separators (e.g. `campaign-overview-chart.png`). No spaces or underscores.
- **Screenshots:** Crop tightly to the relevant area. Annotate with numbered callouts if multiple elements need explaining.
- **Decorative images** (used purely for visual interest, not information) should have empty alt text (`alt=""`) so screen readers skip them.
